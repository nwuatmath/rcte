Your research question will drive the rest of your study. A good question has three building blocks, which we call the "A, B, C's". Click on sections A through C below to see examples, read a description, and build your own question, piece by piece. Be specific! "What are you testing?" can be broken down into three components:

  • The name of the technology you're testing
  • How often users are using it during your study, and for how long (the 'dosage')
  • The way in which it's being implemented, e.g. in small groups versus independently

It is important to name the measure that will be used when testing a tool's effects, if possible. In the case of an intervention for teachers (e.g. additional training), be clear whether the effect you want to measure is on the direct users of the intervention (teachers) or someone else (e.g. students). If you have a particular subgroup of students, teachers or schools in mind, e.g. English learners, include this in the research question.
